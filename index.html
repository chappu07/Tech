Projects

The Humanoid robot

A humanoid robot is a type of robot that is designed to resemble and mimic the form and behavior of the human body. These robots typically have a head, torso, arms, and legs, and they are developed to interact with environments built for humans, perform tasks, and communicate in ways similar to people.

Sumo Bot

A sumo bot is a type of autonomous robot designed to compete in robot sumo competitions. These robots mimic the rules of traditional sumo wrestling, where the objective is to push an opponent's robot out of a defined circular ring, called the dohyo. The robots need to be designed with both offense and defense in mind, as they have to be strong enough to push their opponent while also avoiding being pushed out themselves

Lifi Modules

  • A LiFi module refers to the hardware that enables LiFi a wireless communication technology that uses light to transmit data. Unlike WiFi, which uses radio waves, LiFi utilizes visible light, ultraviolet, or infrared light to provide high-speed communication. The LiFi module typically consists of a light source (such as an LED) and a photodetector to transmit and receive data.

Racer Bot

  • A racer bot is a type of robot specifically designed for speed and agility, typically used in robot racing competitions. The primary goal of a racer bot is to navigate a track as quickly as possible, often competing against other robots to complete laps or reach a finish line first. These bots can be either remotely controlled or autonomous, depending on the competition rules

Frequently Asked Questions

1. What is the main focus of the robotics club?

We design, build, and program robots for competitions and projects, promoting collaboration and skill development.

2. Do I need prior experience to join the club?

No experience is required! We welcome all skill levels and offer mentorship and resources.

3. What types of projects does the club work on??

We work on competition robots, autonomous drones, and custom projects proposed by members.

4. How often does the club meet?

We meet once a week, with extra sessions for projects, workshops, and events.

5. How can I get involved or sign up?

Attend a meeting, sign up on our website, or join our introductory session at the start of the semester.
